2024/25 | CONFIDENTIAL

Designing the future of contactless travel

Year: 2024 | Duration: 11+ months | Role: Senior UX Designer

Overview

I supported a GOV.UK organisation in modernising migration through two distinct workstreams.

Workstreams

One workstream seeks to enhance the current digital immigration status service for millions of global users, and another workstream explores biometric self-enrolment for seamless, passport-less identity verification in the future.

In a nutshell

Our user-centred design (UCD) approach combined Figma mobile prototypes with a Heroku-hosted GOV.UK service, enabling usability testing across diverse demographics, including varying digital literacy levels, age groups, and nationalities.

Notably for workstream one, we achieved a first-time GDS assessment pass, advancing the service to public Beta to support 5+ million users worldwide.

Problem

Workstream 1: Digital immigration status service

For one workstream, users needed a seamless way to prove their immigration status digitally, replacing traditional physical documents. However, key challenges included:

  • Users with limited digital skills struggled with verification
  • Inaccessible content due to complex terminology and acronyms
  • Technology failures leaving users unable to prove their status
  • Users without identity documents facing barriers to account setup

Additionally, the service needed to respond rapidly to policy shifts, such as physical documents expiring in 2024, while ensuring compliance with accessibility standards.

Workstream 2: Biometric self-enrolment trials

For the second workstream, the challenges were:

  • Addressing user trust and security concerns related to biometric data collection and storage
  • Integrating secure biometric authentication processes within wider account creation and management user journeys

Solution

Our approach combined rapid prototyping, usability testing, and policy stakeholder collaboration.

Workstream 1: Digital immigration status service

For the first workstream involving the immigration status service, I did the following:

  • developed a hybrid approach integrating a Figma mobile prototyping with a GOV.UK coded prototype in HTML, CSS, JavaScript to simulate an omni-channel experience
  • tested the service with diverse users across multiple countries, ensuring accessibility for different age groups, digital literacy levels, and technical constraints
  • streamlined content and simplified complex terminology to improve comprehension, particularly for users with limited English skills

Workstream 2: Biometric self-enrolment trials

For the second workstream involving biometric self-enrolment trials, I did the following:

  • designed and coded custom UI components for fingerprint and facial recognition, enhancing the identity verification process with user-centric design principles
  • iterated the prototype based on design crit feedback and user testing

Inclusive design practices were used throughout, giving different options for people who do not have all fingers or cannot scan their face (e.g. for religious reasons).

Process

Workstream 1: Digital immigration status service

  1. User research: Conducted usability tests with diverse user groups to identify pain points and improve digital service accessibility.
  2. Prototyping: Developed iterative prototypes in Figma, refining workflows based on continuous feedback from stakeholders and users.
  3. Accessibility compliance: Ensured compliance with WCAG standards and best practices for digital inclusion.
  4. Collaboration: Worked closely with policy makers, product managers, and development teams to align design solutions with business goals.

Workstream 2: Biometric self-enrolment trials

  1. User research: Engaged participants in biometric trials to understand attitudes towards fingerprint and facial recognition for identity verification.
  2. Prototyping: Created custom UI components for biometric enrolment, iterating based on real-world testing results.
  3. Security & trust: Focused on user trust through transparent communication about biometric data handling and privacy measures.
  4. Cross-functional collaboration: Partnered with security experts, data protection officers, and service designers to ensure robust, future-ready solutions.

Results

Workstream 1: Digital immigration status service

We achieved a first-time GDS assessment pass, allowing the service to move to public Beta.

  • 5+ million users worldwide can now prove their immigration status digitally
  • Simplified content and removal of complex acronyms through inclusive language design
  • Assisted digital support for users who cannot access online services independently

Workstream 2: Biometric self-enrolment trials

Enhanced user trust in biometric authentication systems, achieving 100% user willingness to participate in follow-up enrolment trials.

Conclusion

By adopting a user-centred, omni-channel design approach, we successfully improved the digital immigration status service, ensuring it works for all users, regardless of digital ability or access to technology.

My hybrid Figma and GOV.UK prototyping method allowed rapid iteration, enabling the team to respond swiftly to policy shifts and real-world challenges.

Through dedicated efforts on both digital identity management and biometric enrolment trials, we have significantly advanced migration service design.

This work highlights the balance between security, usability, and accessibility, setting new benchmarks in digital public service transformation.

Next steps

Workstream 1: Digital immigration status service

  • Further enhancements to support users without an identity document, including a video call verification route involving a caseworker
  • Grant funding for local community organisations to assist users with their applications
  • Exploration of additional support models, such as expanding assisted digital webchat services and partnerships with trusted organisations to provide in-person verification support

Workstream 2: Biometric self-enrolment trials

  • Conduct detailed app reviews on suppliers of biometric technologies
  • Scale biometric enrolment trials to support broader demographics and geographic regions
